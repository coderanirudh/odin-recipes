Lasagna

Description

Classic Lasagna, or Lasagne al Forno, is the ultimate Italian comfort food, a masterpiece of layered perfection. It features wide, flat sheets of pasta nestled between a rich, slow-simmered meat sauce (ragu) and a creamy, delicate béchamel. The savory ragu, often made with a blend of beef and pork, is infused with herbs, wine, and tomatoes, creating incredible depth of flavour. The smooth béchamel sauce adds a luxurious creaminess that balances the richness of the meat. To finish, it's crowned with a generous blanket of mozzarella and Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese, then baked until the top is golden-brown and bubbling beautifully. Each slice reveals the distinct, beautiful layers, offering a hearty and deeply satisfying meal that tastes of tradition and love.

Ingredients

    For the Ragu(Meat Sauce)

  • Olive Oil
  • Onion
  • Carrots
  • Celery
  • Ground Beef
  • Ground Pork
  • Red Wine
  • Crushed Tomatoes
  • Tomato Paste
  • Whole Milk
  • Beef Broth
  • Bay Leaf
  • Salt
  • Black Pepper
  • Nutmeg

    For the Bechamel Sauce:

  • Butter
  • All-Purpose Flour
  • Whole Milk
  • Salt
  • White Pepper
  • Nutmeg

    For Assembly:

  • Lasagna Sheets
  • Parmigiano-Reggiano Cheese
  • Low-Moisture Mozzarella Cheese

Recipe Instructions

Step 1: Make the Ragu

  1. saute vegetables

    Heat the olive oil in a large, heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven over medium heat. Add the diced onion, carrots, and celery. Cook, stirring occasionally, for 10-15 minutes until the vegetables are very soft and fragrant.

  2. Brown the Meat:

    Increase the heat to medium-high, add the ground beef and pork to the pot. Break up the meat with a wooden spoon and cook until it's browned all over.

  3. Deglaze and Simmer:

    Pour in the red wine and scrape up any browned bits from the bottom of the pot. Let the wine bubble and cook until it has mostly evaporated.

  4. Combine Ingredients:

    Stir in the tomato paste, followed by the crushed tomatoes, milk, beef broth, bay leaf, salt, pepper, and nutmeg.

  5. Slow Simmer:

    Bring the sauce to a gentle simmer. Once simmering, reduce the heat to low, cover the pot partially, and let it cook for at least 1.5 to 2 hours. Stir occasionally. A longer, slower simmer creates a richer, more developed flavour. The final sauce should be thick. Remove the bay leaf before using.
